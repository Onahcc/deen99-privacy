Deen99
Privacy Policy
Last updated: May 21, 2026

Deen99 ("the app", "we", "us") is a free Islamic companion app developed by Zeeshan Khan as an independent developer. This policy explains what information the app handles and how your privacy is protected.

Short version: Deen99 stores app data on your device and, if you sign in, syncs selected data to Firebase under your account. We do not sell your data, use advertising identifiers, or track you across other apps or services.

1. Information We Collect

Location data

If you grant location permission, the app uses your device's GPS coordinates to calculate accurate prayer times and Qibla direction. Your approximate location may also be used to detect your country so regional prayer calculation methods can be applied automatically.

  • GPS coordinates are processed on your device for prayer time and Qibla calculations.
  • GPS coordinates may be briefly sent to OpenStreetMap's Nominatim service to resolve your city name for display.
  • If you search for a city manually, your search query is sent to Nominatim to find matching places.
  • Your location is not sent to our own servers. It is shared with Nominatim only when needed for city lookup, and is not shared with any third parties for advertising or tracking purposes.

Account information (optional)

If you choose to sign in with Google, we receive the following from your Google account through Google Sign-In and Firebase Authentication:

  • Your display name
  • Your email address
  • Your profile picture URL
  • A unique user ID assigned by Google/Firebase

You can continue to use Deen99 as a guest without signing in. Guest data is stored only on your device unless you later sign in and choose to use synced features.

App data you create or configure

When signed in, the following data you create or configure in the app is synced to Google Firebase (Firestore) under your account so you can access it across devices:

  • Your selected app theme, prayer calculation preferences, and Asr method preference
  • Your notification settings, including which prayers to alert for
  • Your tasbih counts and custom tasbeeh lists
  • Your Qaza prayer tracker settings
  • Your prayer tracker entries, including marked prayers, focus/mood selection, and optional notes you type
  • Your Ramadan mode preference

Some app data is stored locally on your device and may not be synced, such as onboarding state, cached Quran Surahs, Quran bookmarks and continue-reading state, cached or manual location choices, daily goals, custom duas, your Reliable Alerts Mode on/off choice, and local preferences. Guest users' app data is stored in the device's local storage and native preferences.

Quran content requests

When you open a Surah in the Quran reader, Deen99 may request Quran text and translation from external Quran content APIs. The request includes the Surah number being opened. Like any internet request, those providers may receive standard technical information such as your IP address and browser/device network details.

Error and performance data

To help us identify and fix bugs, Deen99 uses Sentry, a third-party error tracking service. When the app encounters an error, the following information may be sent to Sentry:

  • Error message and technical stack trace
  • App release, device/browser details, and operating system information
  • A pseudonymous user identifier, such as your Firebase UID if signed in, or "guest" if not signed in
  • Recent activity breadcrumbs, such as "user switched to Tasbih tab", to help us understand what led to the error

We do not intentionally send your prayer tracker notes, your typed duas, your exact location, your photos, your contacts, or your email address to Sentry. See the Sentry privacy policy for their practices.

2. Information We Do NOT Collect

  • We do not collect or store your browsing history on other apps or websites.
  • We do not use advertising identifiers.
  • We do not show personalized ads.
  • We do not sell your data to anyone, ever.
  • We do not access your contacts, camera, microphone, SMS, or call logs.
  • We do not collect payment card details, bank login details, or in-app payment credentials.

3. Permissions Deen99 Requests

  • Location - Used to calculate accurate prayer times and Qibla direction for your area. You can deny this and enter a location manually if you prefer.
  • Notifications - Used to send prayer time alerts and optional prayer-tracker reminders.
  • Exact Alarms - Used to schedule notifications at the exact prayer time, not an approximate time.
  • Vibration - Used only for notification alerts if vibration is enabled by the device or notification channel.
  • Battery optimization settings - The app may show guidance or open Android battery settings so you can optionally allow more reliable prayer notifications. This is not required by default.
  • Foreground service & ongoing notification (optional, off by default) - If you turn on the optional "Reliable Alerts Mode" inside the app, Deen99 runs an Android foreground service that keeps a permanent low-priority notification visible. This is used only to keep prayer-time alerts firing on time on phones with aggressive battery-saving or background-app restrictions. The ongoing notification displays only the next upcoming prayer's name and time. The service runs entirely on your device, performs no network activity, and does not collect, read, or transmit any personal data. It restarts after a device reboot only while this mode stays enabled, and you can turn it off at any time from inside the app or by tapping "Stop" on the notification.
  • Run after device restart / keep device awake briefly - Used so scheduled prayer alerts can resume after the phone restarts, after app updates, or after time/timezone changes, and so alerts can fire at the exact time. These are used only for prayer-alert scheduling and not for any background data collection.
  • Internet - Used to sync your data if you're signed in, resolve city names, search manual locations, load Quran content, and send crash reports.
  • Device orientation/sensors - Used for the Qibla compass where supported. Sensor readings are used on the device and are not stored by us.
  • Clipboard - Used only when you tap a copy button, such as copying Hadith text or an app link. Clipboard contents are not read or uploaded by the app.

4. Third-Party Services

Deen99 uses the following third-party services. Each has its own privacy practices:

  • Google Firebase Authentication - Provides account login and authentication. Firebase Privacy
  • Google Cloud Firestore - Stores synced app data for signed-in users. Firebase Privacy
  • Google Sign-In - Used for account creation and login. Google Privacy
  • Sentry - Error and crash tracking. Sentry Privacy
  • OpenStreetMap Nominatim - Converts GPS coordinates or manual search queries into city/location names. OSMF Privacy
  • AlQuran Cloud - Provides Quran Arabic text and English translation for the Quran reader. AlQuran Cloud Privacy
  • QuranAPI Pages - Used as a fallback Quran content source if the primary Quran API is unavailable. QuranAPI Pages

5. Data Retention & Deletion

Your data is retained as long as needed to provide the app's features or until you delete it. You can delete your data as follows:

  • Guest data: Uninstalling the app generally removes local app data. Some Android devices may keep certain native preference values depending on device backup or OEM behavior.
  • Signed-in local data: Signing out removes locally cached prayer tracker entries for that signed-in account from the device.
  • Signed-in cloud data: To permanently delete your Firebase/Firestore data, email us using the contact information below. We will delete your cloud data within 30 days.
  • Prayer tracker entries: You can delete individual tracker entries inside the app.
  • Cached Quran/location data, Quran bookmarks, custom duas, and daily goals: These are stored locally to make the app faster and keep your preferences available, and can be removed by clearing app data or uninstalling the app.
  • Error data in Sentry: Error reports are retained according to Sentry project settings and are intended only for debugging and app reliability.

6. Children's Privacy

Deen99 does not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. Parents or guardians who believe their child's data may have been collected can contact us to request deletion.

7. Security

We rely on Google Firebase's security infrastructure to help protect signed-in account data. Data in transit is encrypted using HTTPS. However, no method of transmission or storage over the internet is 100% secure, so we cannot guarantee absolute security.

  • Request deletion of your data
  • Withdraw consent by signing out, disabling permissions, clearing app data, or uninstalling the app
